Hard Style Rules

The non-negotiables.

These aren't suggestions. Write to one real, singular reader — use "you" not "players", and speak from "we".

✅ Always

  • Safeplay (not Safe Play)
  • Signup (not sign-up)
  • 90-ball (not 90 ball)
  • Dab (not daub)
  • Contractions — "don't" not "do not"
  • British spelling conventions
  • Specific numbers, not vague language
  • Use "if" for slot features, not "when"
  • Respond as "we" — never "I"

🚫 Never

  • "Bankroll" — not a British term
  • "Daub" — use "dab"
  • Vague numbers ("a few", "some")
  • Hype language or overpromising
  • AI-sounding or generic copy
  • Guaranteed win language
  • Promos in specific detail
  • "Bonus hunters will love this"
  • "When" for slot features — always "if"
  • Responding as an individual ("I")

📏 On Length

  • Length guidance is always in your copy brief
  • No brief? Shorter is almost always better
  • Emails: each line earns the next
  • Socials: short, conversational, no waffle
  • CTAs: lead with a verb — keep it punchy
Responsible Gambling Context

Know the rules. Write responsibly.

UKGC Stake Limits

The UKGC has set a max stake limit on all online slots: £5 per spin for over 25s and £2 per spin for 18–24 year olds. Always write with this context in mind.

Always Encourage Safe Play

Remind players to stay within their limits. Recommend our safer gambling guide. Encourage use of Safeplay tools. Never use language that implies chasing losses is acceptable.

Complaints

Handling difficult situations.

Every response represents WhichBingo — not you as an individual. Always respond as "we" or "the WhichBingo team."

❌ Wrong

"That's an issue with the operator, not us. We just review sites. You need to contact them directly."

✅ Right

"Hello, I'm the WhichBingo Admin. We're really sorry to hear you're experiencing delays. We completely understand how frustrating slow payouts can be. While we review and rate operators, we don't have direct control over individual casino operations. The best route is to escalate directly with the operator's support. Could you let us know how long you've been waiting? Your experience helps other players make informed choices."

❌ Wrong

"We can't help with account issues — that's between you and the casino."

✅ Right

"We're sorry you're having trouble with your account. While we don't manage accounts directly, we're here to help point you in the right direction. Have you tried reaching out to the operator's live chat? We'd also love to hear more about your experience — it helps us keep our reviews accurate."

❌ Wrong

"Please keep your comments respectful or we'll have to remove them."

✅ Right

"We can hear how frustrated you are, and we're genuinely sorry you've had this experience. We want to help where we can. Could you tell us a bit more about what happened? We take every piece of feedback seriously — it shapes how we review and rate sites."

❌ Wrong

"Our review is correct. We stand by what we've written."

✅ Right

"Thank you for flagging this — we always want our reviews to be as accurate as possible. Could you share more details about what you found? We review sites regularly, but player experiences are invaluable for keeping things up to date. We'll look into this."

❌ Wrong

"We're completely independent. There's no bias in our reviews."

✅ Right

"We understand why transparency matters — it matters to us too. Our reviews are based on hands-on testing and player feedback. We're always open to hearing what's shaped your impression. What specifically gave you that feeling? We'd genuinely like to understand."

❌ Wrong

"We're a small team and get a lot of messages. We'll get to it when we can."

✅ Right

"We're sorry for the delay — that's not the experience we want for you. Thank you for your patience. We're looking into this now and will follow up as soon as we can. In the meantime, is there anything we can help clarify?"

The Golden Rules

For every difficult response.

Can't tick all five? Rewrite it.

💛

Lead with empathy

"We understand," "We're sorry to hear," "That must be frustrating"

🏷️

Identify as WhichBingo

Always "WhichBingo Admin" or "the WhichBingo team"

🔦

Clarify our role

Explain patiently what we do and don't control

🛠️

Offer what we CAN do

Always give something useful, even if it's just guidance

🤝

End supportively

"We hope this gets resolved" — leave the door open

"Own it. Fix it. Learn from it." — When we mess up, our response is a chance to show our values in action.
Channel Guidelines

Same brand. Right format.

The voice stays consistent across every channel.

🖥️

UX Copy

No sales or marketing lingo. Guide the user clearly. Add wit when it entertains without distracting.

Goal: clarity first, personality second
📧

Emails

Get straight to the point. Every line earns the next. Always think about the mood your audience will be in.

Goal: earn every sentence
📱

Socials

Be the friend who actually plays. Empathetic if frustrated, celebratory if winning, informative if confused.

Goal: short, human, real
🔘

CTAs

Lead with verbs. "Find out", "Compare", "Check". Never passive, never vague.

Goal: action, not decoration
📰

News Articles

Know your message and stick to it. Fun for pop culture — factual for regulation topics.

Goal: fun where it fits, factual where it counts
💬

Comments & Reviews

Casual, empathetic and smart. Always clarify who we are. "Hello, I'm the WhichBingo Admin..."

Goal: human, helpful, clear
